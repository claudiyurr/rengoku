About Rengoku

Kyojuro Rengoku is a Demon Slayer of the Demon Slayer Corps and holds the title of 'Flame Hashira'.

The Hashira

Age and Family History

Rengoku is 20 years old in his first appearance, standing at 177 cm tall and weighing around 72 Kg. He is the son of the former Flame Hashira for the Demon Slayer Corps, Shinjuro Renkogu, and is the elder sibling to his brother who also trained alongside him.

Rengoku is well-known amongst his peers for his pure heart, consistent positive attitude and passionate nature. He speaks in a loud and energetic manner, due to rupturing his own eardrums defeating a flute demon. In his free time, he loves to watch sumo wrestling and his favourite food is sweet potatoes with salt grilled bream.

"Go ahead and live with your head held high. No matter how devastated you may be by your own weakness or uselessness, set your heart ablaze."

- Kyojuro Rengoku

Rengoku is considered one of the most powerful and revered swordsman in the organisation. He is preceded by a long lineage of master swordsman and Flame Hashira. Kyojuro became his generation's highest skilled Flame Breathing user, mastering all techniques of the breathing style. These techniques include:

  1. Unknowing Fire - The user charges towards their target at high speeds and unleashes a singular decapitating slash.
  2. Rising Scorching Sun - The user releases an arching sword slash upwards.
  3. Blazing Universe - The user swings their blade downwards in an arc.
  4. Blooming Flame Undulation - The user's blade is swung in a circular motion that is used to defend from incoming attacks or slash multiple targets in one swoop.
  5. Flame Tiger - The user performs a series of powerful sword slashes that seemingly takes the form of a flaming tiger.
  6. Rengoku - The most powerful Flame Breathing technique. The user takes a high stance with their sword before dashing forward at high speeds. The technique is powerful enough to completely carve out the ground in its wake. The user is enveloped in a fiery Japanese dragon as they charge towards the target.
